# Success Metrics

## Purpose

This document defines how Builders Atlas measures success.

Success is not measured by activity.

Success is measured by the creation of trusted, reusable knowledge.

Every roadmap decision should improve one or more of these metrics.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# North Star Metric

## Verified Knowledge Coverage

### Definition

The percentage of real-world knowledge that has been captured, verified,
structured, connected, and kept up to date.

This is the primary success metric for Builders Atlas.

### Formula

Verified Knowledge Coverage =

Verified Entities / Estimated Total Entities

Coverage should be measurable for every entity type.

Examples:

-   Places
-   Villages
-   Beaches
-   Businesses
-   Infrastructure
-   Attractions
-   Organizations
-   Projects
-   Opportunities

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Product Success

Builders Atlas succeeds when the collective knowledge of a place becomes
more complete, more accurate, and more trustworthy over time.

The product does **not** succeed simply because more people signed up or
more records were created.

Knowledge quality is the product.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Primary Success Metrics

## 1. Knowledge Coverage

Measures how much of reality has been documented.

Examples:

-   \% of villages documented
-   \% of restaurants documented
-   \% of beaches documented
-   \% of roads documented
-   \% of hotels documented
-   \% of projects documented

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Knowledge Quality Score

Every entity should receive a quality score.

Factors include:

-   Completeness
-   Verification
-   Freshness
-   Number of Sources
-   Rich Media
-   Connected Relationships

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Verification Rate

Percentage of entities verified by at least two independent
contributors.

Goal:

Increase trust.

Not volume.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. Freshness Score

Measures how recently information has been updated.

Fresh information is more valuable than old information.

Every entity should have:

-   Created At
-   Last Updated
-   Last Verified

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5. Contribution Rate

Measures how much new knowledge is created.

Includes:

-   New entities
-   New observations
-   New photos
-   New updates
-   Corrections
-   Relationship additions

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 6. Community Health

Healthy communities continuously improve knowledge.

Metrics include:

-   Active Contributors
-   Weekly Contributors
-   Returning Contributors
-   Contributor Retention
-   Average Contributions Per Member

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 7. Geographic Coverage

Knowledge should cover the entire region.

Example:

Qeshm

-   East: 81%
-   West: 67%
-   North: 92%
-   South: 54%

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 8. Knowledge Freshness

How much of the database has been updated recently?

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 9. AI Readiness Score

Factors:

-   Structured
-   Connected
-   Verified
-   Rich Context
-   Explainable
-   Machine Readable

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 10. Mission Completion Rate

Every mission should improve:

-   Coverage
-   Verification
-   Freshness
-   Context
-   Relationships
-   Photos

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# What We Do NOT Measure

-   Downloads
-   Registered Users
-   Daily Active Users
-   Followers
-   Likes
-   Impressions
-   Page Views
-   Session Duration

These metrics do not necessarily improve knowledge quality.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Decision Framework

Every product decision should answer:

> Will this improve trusted knowledge?

If the answer is no, reconsider building the feature.
