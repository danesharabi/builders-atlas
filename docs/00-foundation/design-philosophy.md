# Product Principles

## Purpose

This document defines the immutable principles of Builders Atlas.

Every product decision, design decision, engineering decision and AI decision must be evaluated against these principles.

If a new feature violates these principles, it should not be built.

These principles are intentionally difficult to change.

---

# Principle 01

## Community Before Company

Builders Atlas is built by its community.

The platform should empower contributors rather than central administrators.

Knowledge belongs to the community.

The product should encourage contribution instead of dependence on a core team.

---

# Principle 02

## Observation Before Opinion

Builders Atlas stores observations.

Not assumptions.

Not rumors.

Not marketing.

Not personal beliefs.

Every record should be based on:

- observation
- evidence
- measurement
- trusted sources

Whenever uncertainty exists, uncertainty must be visible.

---

# Principle 03

## Trust Before Scale

One trusted record is more valuable than one hundred unreliable records.

Growth should never reduce data quality.

Verification is more important than quantity.

---

# Principle 04

## Capture Once, Reuse Forever

Information should never be entered twice.

Every piece of knowledge should become reusable.

One observation should power:

- AI
- Search
- Reports
- Maps
- Analytics
- Recommendations

---

# Principle 05

## AI Assists

Artificial Intelligence assists contributors.

It never replaces them.

AI may:

- suggest
- classify
- summarize
- detect duplicates
- identify inconsistencies

Humans validate.

Humans approve.

Humans own knowledge.

---

# Principle 06

## Reduce Friction

Every unnecessary click is a bug.

Every unnecessary form field is technical debt.

Every interaction should minimize effort.

Knowledge capture should feel effortless.

---

# Principle 07

## Mobile First

Knowledge is created in the real world.

The primary device is a phone.

Desktop exists for analysis.

Mobile exists for creation.

Every feature should be designed for mobile first.

---

# Principle 08

## Camera Before Keyboard

Photos often contain more information than text.

Whenever possible:

Photo

↓

Location

↓

AI Extraction

↓

User Confirmation

Instead of long forms.

---

# Principle 09

## Location Is The Primary Context

Everything exists somewhere.

Businesses.

Projects.

People.

Events.

Infrastructure.

Observations.

Every object should be connected to geography.

---

# Principle 10

## Objects Before Forms

Users should interact with real-world objects.

Not database tables.

Examples:

Business

Place

Beach

Village

Restaurant

Project

Road

Every screen should represent an object.

Not a spreadsheet.

---

# Principle 11

## Knowledge Is Connected

Knowledge becomes valuable through relationships.

Every entity should connect to others.

Businesses belong to places.

People belong to organizations.

Projects affect locations.

Opportunities depend on infrastructure.

Relationships are first-class citizens.

---

# Principle 12

## Knowledge Evolves

Nothing is permanent.

Everything can improve.

Every record should have:

Version history

Editor history

Source history

Verification history

Knowledge is alive.

---

# Principle 13

## Every Contribution Matters

Small contributions are valuable.

Updating opening hours.

Adding a photo.

Correcting a typo.

Confirming a location.

Everything improves the graph.

---

# Principle 14

## Missing Knowledge Is Valuable Knowledge

The platform should expose what is unknown.

Missing information is a task.

Not a failure.

Unknowns create missions.

---

# Principle 15

## Build For AI From Day One

Every entity should be machine-readable.

Every relationship should be explicit.

Every field should have semantic meaning.

Avoid free text whenever structured data is possible.

---

# Principle 16

## Explainability

Every recommendation should be explainable.

If AI suggests:

"This village has high tourism potential."

Users should understand:

Why.

Which data supports it.

How confident the system is.

---

# Principle 17

## Offline First

Knowledge is often captured where connectivity is poor.

The system must support:

Offline capture.

Background synchronization.

Conflict resolution.

Automatic retry.

---

# Principle 18

## Simplicity Wins

Complexity should exist inside the system.

Never inside the interface.

Users should feel that the product is obvious.

---

# Principle 19

## Long-Term Thinking

Builders Atlas is not built for today's dataset.

It is built to survive decades.

Every decision should consider:

Scalability

Maintainability

Knowledge preservation

Interoperability

Future AI systems

---

# Principle 20

## Build The Collective Memory

The purpose is not collecting data.

The purpose is preserving and evolving collective memory.

Data becomes knowledge.

Knowledge becomes intelligence.

Intelligence creates better decisions.

That is the purpose of Builders Atlas.
